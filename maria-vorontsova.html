    Maria Vorontsova is a Russian pediatric endocrinologist who has served as an advisor to the Russian government in the field of genetic engineering, particularly in the usage of CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats, a family of DNA sequences found in the genomes of prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria and archaea) to create genetically-engineered babies. In 2015, she co-authored a book about ‘idiopathic stunting’ (the medical term used to describe human bodies that are short in stature for which there is no underlying cause) in children (when a child’s height is two standard deviations below the mean predicted height).

    Education

    Maria had attended a German school in Dresden, which was then a part of East Germany, in her kindergarten years. Upon her return to Russia, she joined Peterschule, a primary and secondary school in Saint Petersburg. She had later studied at the German School Moscow, a German-language private school in Moscow.

    After her high school graduation, she joined Saint Petersburg State University, a public research university in Saint Petersburg, to study biology. Maria later studied at Moscow State University, graduating from there in 2011 with a degree in medicine. She was a Ph.D. candidate at the Endocrinology Research Centre, a diagnostic center and research facility in Moscow, and had co-authored 5 medical studies between 2013 and 2015.

    Maria Vorontsova Facts

  • Born in Saint Petersburg (then known as Leningrad), Russia, her family had moved to Dresden, which was then a part of East Germany, when she was a toddler, as her father was posted there at that time. Maria and her family had returned to Saint Petersburg in the spring of 1991.
  • A few years later, when the Tambov Gang was trying to take control of Saint Petersburg’s energy trade leading to several violent gang wars, she and her younger sister Katerina had been moved, temporarily, to Germany. There, the sisters’ legal guardian was Matthias Warnig, who had worked with their father in Dresden as part of a KGB (the main security agency for the Soviet Union from March 1954 until December 1991) cell.
  • In 1995, she had played the violin for a Russian consulate general’s sponsored diplomatic breakfast.
  • The following year, she had nearly died in a fire that had broken out in the sauna of her family’s home. Her father had found her on the 2nd floor of the house, tied a few sheets together, and had convinced her to use them to climb down from the room’s window.
  • To maintain her privacy and ensure that she could lead a life out of the limelight, she changed her name from Mariya Vladimirovna Putina to Maria Vorontsova. The origin of her new surname (Vorontsova), however, is unclear.
