    The passing of longtime E. O. Smith High School basketball coach Ron Pires has left a profound sense of loss within the community of Willimantic, Connecticut. On the receiving end of numerous tributes and prayers, Coach Pires’ impact on his students and the broader community remains a testament to his dedication and commitment to the sport and education.

    Coach Pires began his journey at E.O. Smith in 1980, leaving an indelible mark on the school through his exceptional coaching abilities and remarkable leadership. Renowned for his 35-year tenure, he led the boys basketball team to numerous victories and accolades, including multiple state tournament qualifications and conference championships. His coaching prowess culminated in well-deserved recognition, earning him the title of Connecticut High School Boys Basketball Coach of the Year in 2015.

    Stan Harris posted on Facebook,

    “RIP “Mr. Basketball “ Ron Pires. Thanks for always supporting, mentoring and guiding we young Willimantic boys from the 70’s. You were our coach, teacher and disciplinarian at the YMCA and most of all our FRIEND! Thanks for all you have taught me about basketball sir. This world has lost a great man. You will never be forgotten”

    Beyond his achievements on the court, Ron Pires was known for his unparalleled ability to recognize and nurture the potential of each of his players, fostering not only athletic success but also fostering a deep sense of teamwork and sportsmanship. His leadership style, characterized by a unique blend of empathy and tough love, inspired countless individuals to reach beyond their perceived limits.
