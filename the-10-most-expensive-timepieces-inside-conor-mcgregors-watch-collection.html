    Conor McGregor was interested in sports from a young age and had a particular interest in football before he began training for boxing at 12 years of age. While being a plumber’s apprentice, he met UFC fighter Tom Egan who soon began training him. With John Kavanagh, McGregor trained in various sports, including karate, kickboxing, and jiu-jitsu, earning him the agility and strength to complete mixed martial arts contests. After making his MMA debut at 18, UFC signed him in 2013 via a multi-fight contract. Within two years, McGregor made $5 million in paydays from his matches and $85 million for his fight against Floyd Mayweather. The professional fighter has amassed a massive net worth of $200 million.

    Conor McGregor is known to make flashy expenses and spends a fortune buying stunning real estate properties and adding cars to his collection. He is also interested in buying exclusive watches from notable brands such as Rolex, Jacob& Co., and Patek Philippe. A collection worth millions, let’s look at the expensive watches owned by Conor McGregor.

    Rolex Day-Date 40 ($52,650)

    One of Conor McGregor’s favorite timepiece models, he is often seen wearing a Rolex Day-Date 40 in various shades and customizations. According to HypeBeast, he first showed off his Day-Date 40 in Everose Gold during a pre-fight press conference For UFC 257 in 2021. He also owns the same watch in yellow gold with an olive green dial and white gold with a diamond dial.

    Patek Philippe Nautilus ($360,000)

    Patek Philippe Nautilus is a famous watch amongst athletes who want to combine style with sophistication. The Nautilus has been in production since 1976 and remains timeless even today. McGregor often pairs his yellow gold timepiece with shorts and shows off the expensive watch on his Instagram.

    Patek Philippe Ellipse ($49,983)

    Conor McGregor combined work with fashion when he wore the Patek Philippe Ellipse for his training sessions. He shared a montage of shots on his Instagram in late 2020. The classic watch was released in 2019 and came with a gold case with a brown leather strap. Its technology displays the time zones of 24 major countries at once.

    Patek Philippe Annual Calendar Chronograph Limited Edition ($100,000)

    Weir & Sons celebrated their 150th anniversary by introducing the limited-edition Patek Philippe Annual Calendar Chronograph in 2020, as noted by Robb Report. While the regular Annual Calendar costs $51,000, the Limited Edition timepiece was double its price, and only 25 were produced. Made to commemorate the special relationship with Ireland’s Weir & Sons, the Dublin-born star was first to get his hands on the watch.

    Rolex Cosmograph Daytona Eye of the Tiger ($460,409)

    Introduced in the 1970s, the Daytona Eye of the Tiger is one of the most exclusive timepieces sold by Rolex. McGregor often wears the timepiece during his practice sessions in the boxing ring and lounging during a vacation. Made from solid yellow gold, it features a diamond-encrusted bezel and a diamond-patterned detailing.

    Patek Philippe Calatrava Skeleton ($84,500)

    One of his favorite timepieces, the Patek Philippe Calatrava, owned by McGregor, is made from 18-carat rose gold and has engraved motifs to enhance the dial. While the professional fighter often wears the timepiece during photoshoots, he was famously styled with the Calatrava Skeleton at the 2022 Cannes Film Festival while celebrating an event with Chopard Jewelers.

    Jacob & Co. Astronomia ‘Casino’ ($620,000)

    While celebrating his 32nd birthday in style in 2020, Conor McGregor received a lifetime gift when he got the Astronomia Casino by watchmaker Jacob & Co., one of his favorite brands. As mentioned by Bleacher Report, the professional fighter took Instagram to show off his gift, which featured sapphire apertures, and an 18-carat rose gold dial.

    Patek Philippe World Time ($64,700)

    Conor McGregor was first spotted wearing the minimalist Patek Philippe World Time during his vacation in Monaco in 2020. Lounging on a yacht, he posted several photos from his vacation for his Instagram followers. The rose gold watch is a simple yet elegant timepiece that comes with a skeleton dial.

    Jacob & Co. Astronomia Tourbillon Baguette ($1 million)

    UFC rivalries have always been memorable, and to celebrate his rematch with fellow opponent Dustin Poirier, McGregor splashed out on an Astronomia Tourbillon Baguette with a sapphire case. It has a set of 342 baguette diamonds, 288-faceted Jacob-cut diamonds, and a hand-painted Earth globe that rotates continuously, as stated by Forbes. McGregor reportedly went in his new Rolls Royce to grab the purchase.

    Jacob & Co. Rasputin Diamond Erotic Minute Repeater ($2.3 million)

    The most expensive watch in his collection, Conor McGregor, had his eyes set on the Rasputin Diamond from the minute he purchased the Astronomia Tourbillon Baguette. The white gold watch has over 500 diamonds, and with the automaton moving feature, the minute repeater chimes the time. It is also a revealing watch, showcasing an erotic scene on the dial.

    Conor McGregor has curated a stunning collection of timepieces featuring unique features and designs. His watches are hard to miss on his popular Instagram posts, made from pure gold in various shades. As the professional fighter continues to play, it won’t be a surprise to see a rare new watch on his wrist sometime soon.
