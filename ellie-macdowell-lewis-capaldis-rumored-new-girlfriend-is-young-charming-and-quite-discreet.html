    Career

    As part of The Scottish Music Centre’s Hit the Road project, Capaldi went on a three-stop tour in 2014. He played with Jacob and Rory Green, as well as Zo Bestel, in Dumfries, Edinburgh, and Fort William. When he was 18, his manager Ryan Walter found him through an iPhone recording he made in his bedroom and put on his SoundCloud page. The day after Walter first talked to Capaldi, he flew from the United States to Britain to see him play live. Capaldi graduated from New College Lanarkshire, Motherwell, with an HND in music in 2016.

    On his first extended play record, Bloom EP, which came out on October 20, 2017, he worked with Grammy-winning producer Malay, who has worked with Frank Ocean for a long time. His first song, “Bruises,” came out on March 31, 2017. The song got played nearly 28 million times on Spotify around the world in a short amount of time, making him the fastest unsigned artist to ever reach 25 million plays on the platform. Soon after that, he was signed to the German branch of Universal Music Group and put in charge of the Vertigo Berlin division. In the UK, Virgin EMI Records sells his records, and in the US, Capitol Records does so.

    Late in 2017, Capaldi was named one of Vevo dscvr’s “Artists to Watch in 2018,” and at the Scottish Alternative Music Awards, he won Best Acoustic. He was also on the short list for BBC Music’s 2018 Sound of.

    In November 2017, he opened for Rag’n’Bone Man on his European tour. In January 2018, he opened for Milky Chance on the North American leg of their Blossom tour. Some famous people, like Chlo Grace Moretz, Ellie Goulding, and Niall Horan, paid attention to him. Horan then asked Capaldi to join him on two dates of his Flicker World Tour in March 2018 at the Glasgow SEC Armadillo. In May 2018, Capaldi went on the Thrill of It All European Tour with Sam Smith. He opened for Smith on 19 dates. After that, he announced a fourth headlining tour of the UK and Europe. This time, he would play venues with a capacity of 2,000 people across the UK and Europe, including Glasgow’s Barrowland Ballroom for two nights, where both shows sold out.

    On July 13, 2018, Capaldi was named as one of two acts on BBC Radio 1’s Brit List. This gave him a spot on Radio 1’s playlist for the next three weeks.

    In August 2018, Irish Indie rock band Kodaline asked Capaldi to open for them at a concert in Belfast. During the summer of 2018, Capaldi was also on the lineups for Lollapalooza, Bonnaroo, Firefly, Mountain Jam, Osheaga, Reading & Leeds Festival, Rize, and TRNSMT, among others. Capaldi’s second extended play, Breach, came out on November 8, 2018. It had “Tough” and “Grace,” which had been released as singles before, as well as “Someone You Loved” and a demo of “Something Borrowed,” which were both new songs.
