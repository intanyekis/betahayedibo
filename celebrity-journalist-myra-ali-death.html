Myra Ali’s passing has left a void in the hearts of many, as she was a beacon of inspiration, courage, and resilience.

    Who was Myra Ali?

    Myra Ali was not just a journalist; she was a force to be reckoned with. Known for her impactful writing and advocacy for diversity and body positivity, Myra used her platform to give voice to those often overlooked. Despite living with a disability, she never let it define her. Instead, she used it as a catalyst to drive change and spark conversations about disabled women and their representation.

    Myra Ali Career

    Throughout her career, Myra wrote #1 trending articles for numerous esteemed publications, including Forbes, British GQ, Marie Claire, Guardian, Huffington Post, Stylist, Metro, New York Post, and Cosmopolitan. Her writings were not just informative, but they served as a catalyst for change and dialogue about diversity, body positivity, and the experiences of disabled women of color.

    Her contributions to journalism and advocacy led to invitations from prestigious institutions like the University of Cambridge to participate in panel discussions on Women in Journalism. She also served as a guest editor for BBC 5 Live, further amplifying her influence and impact.
